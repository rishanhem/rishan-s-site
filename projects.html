Momentum Robotics Lab · Carnegie Mellon

At Carnegie Mellon’s Momentum Robotics Lab, I lead RobotRemote, an ARKit-powered system that turns any iPhone into a high-fidelity teleoperation controller. By translating phone pose data into robot joint movements, we outperform legacy GPS and gyroscope approaches in accuracy and latency.

I architected the Swift networking stack, realistic Blender-based digital twin, and the mobile UI that lets remote operators see both their live motion and the robot’s response in real time.

Highlights

  • Engineered the RobotRemote prototype with Swift’s Network framework, streaming pose (x, y, z) and pitch/yaw/roll into a Blender-based robotic twin.
  • Designed an intuitive mobile interface that visualizes operator motion and arm response in near real time.
  • Secured IRB approval and am co-running HRI user studies to benchmark usability and precision against traditional control setups.

Learning Directed OS · UT Austin

At UT Austin’s LDOS lab, I contributed to an NSF-funded effort exploring machine learning–optimized operating systems. Working under PhD candidate Aditya Tewari, I trained Perceptron and KNN models that predict optimal kernel scheduling decisions.

My models reached 92% accuracy with strong recall and F1 scores, becoming the benchmark among high school researchers and informing the next wave of large-scale model integration.

Highlights

  • Implemented cross-validation pipelines to ensure clean train/test separation and robust generalization.
  • Delivered the most accurate kernel model in cohort, guiding future exploration into scaling LDOS with additional algorithms.
  • Collaborated on documentation so the team can port my workflow into production-ready schedulers.

Texas A&M Kingsville · Separation Research

Simulated the separation of a 50/50 wt% acetone-water mixture using methyl isobutyl ketone (MIBK) as the extracting agent, pairing liquid-liquid extraction with DSTWU and RadFrac distillation models.

The work supported industrial purification scenarios requiring high-purity feedstock and was selected for presentation at the U.S. Department of Education MSEIP Conference.

Highlights

  • Modeled both DSTWU and rigorous RadFrac columns to compare purity, recovery, and energy tradeoffs.
  • Optimized solvent ratios and tray counts to meet industrial-grade purity thresholds.
  • Documented the workflow so partner labs can replicate the two-step approach.
